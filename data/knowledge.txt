Loan Risk Assessment Guidelines

A loan application is considered LOW RISK when:
- Credit score is above 650
- Debt-to-income ratio is below 0.4
- The applicant has been employed for more than 2 years
- Loan amount is less than 3x annual income
- Age is between 25 and 60
- No previous loan defaults

A loan application is considered HIGH RISK when:
- Credit score is below 550
- Debt-to-income ratio is above 0.5
- The applicant has been employed for less than 1 year
- Loan amount exceeds 5x annual income
- History of missed payments or defaults
- Income is unstable or unverified

Borderline cases (MEDIUM RISK):
- Credit score between 550 and 650
- Debt-to-income ratio between 0.4 and 0.5
- Employment between 1 and 2 years
- These cases require manual review

Key factors in loan decision:
1. Credit Score - most important factor
2. Debt-to-Income Ratio - measures repayment capacity
3. Employment History - measures income stability
4. Loan Amount vs Income - measures affordability
5. Age - measures earning potential
